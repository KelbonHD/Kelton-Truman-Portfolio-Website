Introduction

Hello! My name is Kelton Lamar Truman, and I am currently studying and trying to become proficient in software engineering. There's a lot I need to know, but I'm up to the task and have been working at it for months now.
I am currently studying at my own pace through Codecademy's Software Engineer course, and hope to have it done before I leave the Navy.

Before Software Engineering

High School

When I was in high school, I went through many different phases of what I wanted to do. Chemical Engineering, Automotive Repair, and yes, I even considered Web Development! Basically anything I was inherently good at, I latched onto, and wanted to pursue.
After seeing the price of college/university tuition, I wanted nothing to do with any of it! So what did I do? I joined the United States Navy.

U.S Navy

I initally went into the Navy hoping to become an ET, which stands for Electronic Technician. Basically, I would be working on anything and everything that has electricity surging through it on ships, carriers, you name it. I liked the idea of it, and though I would be good at it, but then my recruiter proposed an idea to me.
He saw my ASVAB scores, which are scores used to judge which rates you qualify for, and saw they were high enough that I qualified for another test. This test was the DLAB, a language capabilities test used to recruit linguists.

I decided I would take it, not thinking much of it. The lowest passing score was 104, and that was the score I got. That's when they offered me the new rate of CTI, standing for Cryptologic Interpretive Technician. They told me the benifits of such a rate, and I accepted in the end.

I worked my way through boot camp, and was assigned the Persian Farsi language, which I didn't know existed at the time. I went through the Defense Language Institue and graduated with a 3/2+/1+, with a 3/3/2 being the highest score.
After that I moved on to learn the operational side of my job, and have worked with NSA since mid 2019 doing things I really can't talk about! But, I've gained a lot of experience in cyber security and general computer knowledge on top of my already aquired skills.

My Future After the Navy

Currently, I'm still in the Navy. But I thought, being a little less than a year out from separation from the Navy, I should start looking towards the future. This was tricky, as I didn't think I had any skills to get me anywhere outside the Navy.
But then I remembered, back in highschool I had a huge aptitude for web development and coding in general. Programming and Web Development were the two classes I excelled above everyone constantly, and easily picked up on.
That is when I looked up the possibilites, and chose Software Engineering as the best fit for me.

Present Day

17 Months Out from Separation

As of now, it's been about 6-7 months since I've started Software Engineering courses. It's been tricky, trying to balance my work life and other responsibilites on top of learning this. But I've been doing it, 30% through the Codecademy Software Engineering Course as of now.
I've mostly been using Codecademy as an introduction into software engineering, and as a "warm-up" for a more accredited, full-time course outside of the Navy.

I'm hoping to go through Thinkful, an accredited, 1 on 1 mentoring online class experience, where I can learn Software Engineering Mon-Fri full time, until course completion. After this, I'm hopeful to land a nice job to be able to support my significant other and I. That's all there is to it!
